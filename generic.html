About Me

Software Enigineer,
Using Coding to change the World.

As an entry-level software engineer, I bring a unique perspective to the tech industry. Growing up with immigrant parents, I learned the value of hard work, perseverance, and determination. My mother always instilled in me the belief that "people can take anything from you except for your skills," and this has been a driving force behind my decision to pursue a career in technology. As the first person in my family to break generational curses and pursue a career in the tech industry, I am committed to creating a better future for myself and my family. I am passionate about using my technical skills to solve real-world problems and make a positive impact on society. During my academic career, I have gained experience in various programming languages, including Python, Java, and JavaScript, and have designed and developed several projects utilizing full-stack web development technologies. As a team player, I enjoy working collaboratively with others to bring innovative ideas to life. I thrive in fast-paced environments where I can learn new skills and take on new challenges. I am excited to join a team of like-minded individuals who share my passion for technology and making a positive impact on the world.

I Develop high-quality software solutions using a variety of programming languages, frameworks, and tools Collaborate with clients and cross-functional teams to identify project requirements and deliver solutions that meet business needs Conduct thorough testing and debugging to ensure the stability, functionality, and usability of software products Continuously learn and incorporate new technologies and methodologies to stay at the forefront of the industry Participate in code reviews and contribute to the improvement of team development processes Write clear and concise documentation to facilitate the use and maintenance of software products Communicate effectively with team members and stakeholders to ensure project goals are met on time and within budget."
